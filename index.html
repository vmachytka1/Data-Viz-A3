Overarching Question

I would like to answer the following quesiton: How has the population distribution at the extremities (very young, defined as 0-4 years, and very old, defined as 85+ years) evolved from 1900 to 2000, and what are the differences in these changes between genders?

My understanding is that significant advancements in public health and medical care impacted life expectancy and mortality rates. I would expect high infant mortality rates and relatively short life expectancies due to infectious diseases in the early 1900s. With improvements in sanitation and vaccines, more individuals reached advanced age. As a result, by the year 2000, a larger proportion of the population was able to live into their 80s and beyond. I would also be curious to observe how societal shifts have impacted family sizes and measure what proportion of the population consists of young individuals in 2000 vs in 1900. I am guessing that these trends will likely manifest differently across genders, especially given the differences in health outcomes and life expectancy between males and females. My goal for this exercise will be to visualize these shifts to better understand the reshaped distribution of age and gender structure of the American population.
